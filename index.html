The Institute of Symmetry

Measuring Human Symmetry. Building the Evidence.

Human symmetry is not merely aesthetic. It is a measurable property of biological organization that reflects how the body distributes mechanical loads, adapts over time, and preserves functional capacity.

The Institute of Symmetry was established to create the first evidence-based infrastructure for objective symmetry assessment and longitudinal observation of structural human adaptation.

Why It Matters

Most healthcare systems evaluate diseases.
Very few evaluate the structural organization of the human body itself.

Our goal is to build the scientific infrastructure required to measure structural adaptation objectively — rather than describe it subjectively.
